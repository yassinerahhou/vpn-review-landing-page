Why Use a VPN Service?

A Virtual Private Network (VPN) is an internet security service that allows users to access the internet as if they were connected to a private network. VPNs use encryption to create a secure connection on an unsecured internet infrastructure.

How Do VPNs Help Secure Data?

Encryption is a way of scrambling data so that only authorized parties can understand the information. It takes readable data and modifies it so that it appears random to hackers or anyone else who intercepts it. In this sense, encryption is a kind of "secret code".

A VPN works by establishing encrypted connections between devices. All devices connecting to the VPN establish encryption keys, and these keys are used to encode and decode all the information sent between them.

Thanks to this encryption, VPN connections remain private even if they travel over a public internet infrastructure. Imagine Jean is shopping online from his hotel room. Now suppose a criminal has secretly infiltrated the hotel's network infrastructure and is monitoring all the data that passes through it (like a tapped phone line). Jean's data is still secure thanks to the VPN. All the criminal can see is the encrypted version of the data.

When you make a transaction or need to send or receive sensitive information, a VPN enhances your security.

Do I Need a VPN on All My Devices?

Yes, you will need to install a VPN client on each device you want to connect to the VPN.

Fortunately, all the VPNs recommended on our site allow you to connect multiple devices with a single account.

So, whether you have a Windows PC, a laptop, a Macbook, an iPhone, or an Android phone, you can easily connect to your VPN account with any device without having to open another account.
